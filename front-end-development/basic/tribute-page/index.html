Pep Guardiola

The man who revolutionized Football

Pep Guardiola celebrating the 2009 Champions League win with his FC Barcelona players.

Here are some major events in Pep Guardiola's life

  • 1971 - Born 18 January in Santpedor, Barcelona
  • 1984 - Aged 13, Guardiola joins the famous La Masia academy of FC Barcelona
  • 1990 - 'Pep' makes his debut for the senior side against Cádiz CF
  • 1991 - Guardiola plays a key part in the team's domestic and European Cup successes
  • 2001 - Announces his desire to leave the Camp Nou after 17 years. His next move sees him travel to Brescia in Italy.
  • 2003 - After an unhappy stay in Italy Guardiola follows Gabriel Batistuta to Qatar
  • 2005 - After moving to Mexico for a brief spell with Dorados de Sinaloa, he hangs up his boots at the end of the season
  • 2008 - Starts coaching career as manager of Barcelona's B side with Tito Vilanova as his assistant. The pair lead the team to promotion in their first year in charge
  • 2008 - Barcelona's president, Joan Laporta, announces that Guardiola is the man to replace Frank Rijkaard as first-team coach
  • 2009 - Barça go on to win every trophy they compete for in the calendar year 2009
  • 2010 - After securing the La Liga title once again Barça beat Manchester United 3-1 at Wembley to win yet another Champions League title
  • 2012 - Decides to take a one-year sabbatical in New York
  • 2013 - Guardiola is announced as the new manager of Bayern Munich for the start of the 2013-14 season
